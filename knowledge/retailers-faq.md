# General FAQ — InsureFlow Retailers Insurance

## What is retailers insurance?
Retailers insurance is a commercial package policy designed for retail stores. It combines property cover for the store's stock, contents, fixtures, fittings and equipment against fire, theft and damage, with a general liability section that protects the business against third-party injury and property-damage claims arising from its operations.

A typical retail package also contemplates the kinds of exposures a store faces day to day — public liability for customers on the premises, products liability for goods sold, and the financial impact of an insured event that interrupts trading. InsureFlow's package is driven by the value of stock and contents (the sum insured) for the property section and by annual turnover for the liability section.

## Who needs retailers insurance?
This policy suits owners and operators of retail premises, including:
- Apparel and footwear stores
- Electronics retailers
- Grocery and convenience stores
- Furniture and homeware stores
- Hardware stores
- Restaurants and cafes
- Other retail storefronts

Cannabis retail is also recognised but is treated as a regulated specialist class (see referral rules below).

## How is the premium calculated?
The annual premium is driven by the **stock and contents value** (your sum insured) at a base rate of **1.5%**, then adjusted by a series of multipliers and a flat general liability loading:

- **Store type** — e.g. apparel (0.95), grocery (1.05), electronics (1.20), restaurant (1.25), cannabis (1.40)
- **Province/location** — e.g. PE (0.95), MB/NS/SK (1.05), AB (1.08), QC (1.10), ON (1.12), BC (1.15)
- **Building construction** — masonry/concrete (0.90), mixed (1.00), wood frame (1.30)
- **Fire protection** — sprinklered + monitored alarm (0.85), monitored alarm only (1.00), extinguishers only (1.25)
- **Burglar alarm** — central station (0.90), local only (1.15)
- **Prior claims (5 yrs)** — none (0.95), 1 claim (1.15), 2 claims (1.35)
- **Deductible** — see options below

A flat **general liability loading** is then added based on annual turnover band:
- Under $250,000 turnover: $350 CAD
- $250,000 to under $1,000,000: $600 CAD
- $1,000,000 to under $5,000,000: $1,200 CAD
- $5,000,000 and above: $2,500 CAD

All amounts are in CAD.

## What coverage limits are available?
The property sum insured is the **total value of stock and contents** you declare, from a minimum of $10,000 CAD up to $20,000,000 CAD. A stock and contents sum insured exceeding $5,000,000 CAD requires senior underwriter approval and a survey of protections. The general liability section is provided alongside the property cover and is priced by your annual turnover band.

## What deductible options are available?
You can choose from the following deductibles (the higher the deductible, the lower the premium):
- $1,000 CAD (higher premium)
- $2,500 CAD (balanced — most popular)
- $5,000 CAD (lower premium)
- $10,000 CAD (lowest premium)

## What can cause a quote to be declined or referred?
**Declined (outside underwriting appetite):**
- A retail premises with **no fire protection** of any kind

**Referred for individual underwriter review:**
- Stores in the **Northwest Territories, Nunavut, or Yukon** (remoteness and limited fire/emergency response)
- **Cannabis retail** (regulated specialist class — licensing and security controls reviewed)
- A stock and contents **sum insured over $5,000,000 CAD** (senior approval and survey of protections)
- A premises with **no burglar alarm** (theft controls reviewed)
- **Three or more claims** within the last five years
