# General FAQ — InsureFlow Rental Home (Landlord) Insurance

## What is rental home (landlord) insurance?
Rental home (landlord) insurance is a specialist policy for a tenanted residential property. It insures the building (and landlord-owned contents) against fire, water, theft, and liability perils. A standard owner-occupier home policy is generally not valid once a property is let to tenants, so a dedicated landlord policy is required.

In addition to physical damage to the structure, landlord cover typically responds to landlord liability (claims by tenants or visitors arising from the property) and can include loss of rental income if an insured event makes the property uninhabitable. Cover applies while the property is occupied by tenants — a currently vacant property is not eligible and should be insured under the Vacant Home product instead.

## Who needs it?
You may need this policy if:
- You own a detached house, semi-detached/townhouse, or condo unit that you rent out
- You own a small multi-unit building (2–4 units)
- You let to long-term families, professionals, students, or subsidized/DSS tenants
- You are a landlord moving from an owner-occupier policy now that the property is tenanted

## What provinces do you operate in?

## What information do I need to get a quote?
- Applicant (landlord) name, phone, and email
- Province of the rental property
- Property type (detached, semi/townhouse, condo unit, multi-unit 2–4, or apartment block 5+)
- Year the property was built
- Estimated rebuild cost (replacement/reconstruction cost of the building — this becomes your sum insured)
- Tenant profile (long-term family, professionals, students, short-term/Airbnb, or subsidized/DSS)
- Current occupancy (fully occupied, partially occupied, or vacant)
- Number of property claims in the last 5 years
- Whether working smoke and carbon-monoxide alarms are present throughout the property
- Preferred deductible

## How is the premium calculated?
The annual premium is driven by the sum insured. It starts from a base rate of 0.4% of the rebuild value, then is adjusted by a series of risk multipliers and flat loadings:
- Province of risk
- Property type
- Property age (based on year built)
- Tenant profile
- Occupancy status
- Prior claims history (last 5 years)
- Working smoke/CO alarms
- Deductible selected

Two flat dollar loadings may also apply: a $200 CAD partial-vacancy loading if the property is only partially occupied, and a $150 CAD student-let loading for student tenancies. The premium is also shown as a monthly figure (annual premium divided by 12).

## What coverage limits are available?
Your sum insured is the estimated rebuild cost of the building, which you enter when quoting. It must be between $75,000 and $5,000,000 CAD, and should reflect the replacement/reconstruction cost — not the market or land value. A rebuild value above $2,000,000 CAD is referred for senior underwriter approval and may require a property survey.

## What deductible options are available?
You can choose from four deductibles (CAD):
- $1,000 — higher premium
- $2,500 — balanced and most popular
- $5,000 — lower premium
- $10,000 — lowest premium

A higher deductible lowers your premium but increases your out-of-pocket cost per claim.

## What can cause a quote to be declined or referred?
The following are referred to an underwriter for individual review:
- A property in the Northwest Territories, Nunavut, or Yukon (remoteness and limited response infrastructure)
- An apartment block of 5 or more units (commercial habitational risk)
- A property built before 1950 (review of wiring and plumbing updates)
- A rebuild value exceeding $2,000,000 CAD (senior approval; possible survey)
- Short-term / Airbnb lettings (commercial occupancy exposure)
- A currently vacant property — not eligible for landlord cover; use the Vacant Home product instead
- Three or more property claims within the last 5 years
- Missing or non-working smoke/CO alarms (working alarms are required throughout the property)
