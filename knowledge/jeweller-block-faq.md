# General FAQ — InsureFlow Jeweller's Block Insurance

## What is Jeweller's Block insurance?
Jeweller's Block is a specialist commercial policy that covers a jewellery business's stock — finished jewellery, loose gemstones, and precious metals — against theft, burglary, robbery, and accidental damage. Unlike a standard commercial property policy, it follows the stock wherever it goes: on the premises, locked in the safe or vault, displayed in windows, and while in transit to trade shows, customers, or deliveries.

Because jewellery stock is high-value, portable, and a frequent target for crime, insurers underwrite these risks closely around the physical security of the premises (rated safes, monitored alarms, window discipline) and the trading profile of the business.

## Who needs it?
This policy is intended for businesses that hold or handle jewellery stock, including:
- Retail jewellers
- Wholesalers and dealers
- Manufacturers
- Pawnbrokers
- Online-only sellers

## What provinces do you operate in?
InsureFlow writes Jeweller's Block across Canada — all provinces and territories: Alberta, British Columbia, Manitoba, New Brunswick, Newfoundland and Labrador, Northwest Territories, Nova Scotia, Nunavut, Ontario, Prince Edward Island, Quebec, Saskatchewan, and Yukon. Businesses in the Northwest Territories, Nunavut, and Yukon are referred to an underwriter for individual review due to remoteness and limited security and response infrastructure.

- Contact name, phone, and email

- Number of years the business has been trading
- Maximum value of stock on the premises at any one time (this becomes your sum insured)
- What portion of stock is locked in a rated safe or vault overnight
- The grade of safe or vault protecting the stock
- The type of burglar alarm protecting the premises
- How much stock value is left in display windows after hours
- Whether stock is regularly taken off the premises, and the maximum value carried off-site
- The number of theft, burglary, or robbery losses in the last five years (with the type, repair status, and size for each)
- The deductible you prefer
- What the bulk of the stock consists of (watches, gold/precious metals, mixed jewellery, or diamonds/loose stones)
- What portion of stock is held on consignment or memo (owned by others)
- The value of the single most valuable item held
- How entry to the premises is controlled during trading hours
- What CCTV coverage protects the premises
- The number of employees (including owners)
- Whether jewellery repairs or manufacturing are carried out on the premises

## How is the premium calculated?
The premium is driven by your **sum insured** — the maximum value of stock on the premises at any one time. The base annual premium is **1.0% of the sum insured**, then adjusted by risk multipliers and flat dollar loadings:

- **Business type** — retail is the baseline; wholesalers, manufacturers, pawnbrokers, and online-only sellers carry higher factors
- **Province / location** — weighted for metro property-crime and smash-and-grab exposure
- **Trading experience** — established traders (10+ years) earn a discount; newer ventures carry a loading
- **Stock secured overnight** — the more stock locked in a rated safe or vault, the lower the rate
- **Safe / vault grade** — high-grade vaults attract the best rate
- **Burglar alarm** — central-station monitoring with safe contacts earns the best terms
- **Window exposure** — emptying windows nightly reduces the rate
- **Off-premises exposure** — applied only when stock is carried off-site
- **Loss history** — prior theft/burglary/robbery losses increase the premium
- **Deductible** — a higher deductible lowers the premium
- **Principal stock** — diamonds/loose stones carry the highest factor; watches the lowest
- **Consignment / memo stock** — more stock held on consignment raises the rate
- **Entry control** — mantraps, buzzer entry, or an on-site guard earn a discount; an open door adds a surcharge
- **CCTV coverage** — full recorded coverage earns the best rate
- **Staff headcount** — more employees increase the internal-theft exposure
- **On-premises repairs** — a workshop on site adds a loading

Two flat loadings (CAD) may also apply: a **$500** loading when more than $50,000 is left in display windows after hours, and a **$250** transit loading when stock is regularly carried off-premises.

## What coverage limit (sum insured) is available?
Coverage equals the maximum value of stock on the premises at any one time, declared at quote. The sum insured can range from **$25,000 to $20,000,000 CAD**. A maximum stock value exceeding **$5,000,000 CAD** requires senior underwriter approval and a survey of the protections.

## What deductible options are available?
Standard deductibles are **$2,500, $5,000, $10,000, and $25,000 CAD**. The $5,000 deductible is the most popular, balanced option; a higher deductible lowers your premium but increases your out-of-pocket cost per claim.

## What can cause a quote to be declined or referred?
A quote is **declined** (outside underwriting appetite) when:
- No stock is locked in a rated safe or vault overnight
- There is no burglary-rated safe or vault on the premises
- There is no burglar alarm on the premises

A quote is **referred** to an underwriter for individual review when:
- The business is a pawnbroker or an online-only seller
- The business is located in the Northwest Territories, Nunavut, or Yukon
- The business has been trading less than one year
- The maximum stock value exceeds $5,000,000 CAD
- Less than half of the stock is kept in a rated safe overnight
- Protection relies on a locking cabinet or showcase alone (no rated safe)
- The premises has only a local audible alarm without central-station monitoring
- More than $50,000 of stock is left in display windows after hours
- More than $100,000 of stock is carried off-premises at any one time
- The business has had three or more theft, burglary, or robbery losses in the last five years, the security weaknesses behind a prior loss remain uncorrected, or a single prior loss exceeded $500,000 CAD
- More than 50% of stock is held on consignment / memo
- The single most valuable item is worth over $500,000 CAD (needs a per-item sub-limit)
- The premises has no CCTV (recorded camera coverage is expected for this class)
