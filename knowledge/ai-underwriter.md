# AI Underwriter Recommendation

## What is the AI underwriter recommendation?
On a referred quote, an underwriter or admin can click **Get AI Recommendation**. An AI underwriter reads the full application details, the product type, the premium and coverage, and the specific reasons the quote was referred, then returns a structured advisory verdict.

## What does the verdict include?
- **Recommendation** — approve or decline.
- **Confidence** — low, medium, or high.
- **Summary** — a one-to-two sentence rationale.
- **Reasons** — a few short, specific underwriting reasons.

The recommendation also pre-fills the review note so the underwriter can adjust and confirm.

## Is the decision automated?
No. The AI recommendation is **advisory only**. A human underwriter always makes the final approve/decline decision. The AI assists and speeds up the review; it does not bind or decline on its own.

## How does it weigh the risk?
It weighs the referral reasons most heavily (those are why a human was asked to look), then considers the risk holistically — occupancy/vacancy, construction and age, heating (especially solid-fuel/wood), electrical and plumbing, protection and security, loss history, and coverage adequacy versus value. It is deliberately conservative: thin or missing information, or a poor risk, leans toward decline.

## What powers it?
The recommendation is generated by a large language model through the broker portal's configured AI key. The engine is pluggable, so the underlying model can be upgraded over time without changing the review workflow.

## What if it's not configured?
If the AI key isn't set up in the environment, the button returns a "not configured" message and the underwriter simply reviews manually — the rest of the review flow is unaffected.

## Who can use it?
Only underwriters and admins, and only on quotes that have been referred. Brokers do not see the AI recommendation.
