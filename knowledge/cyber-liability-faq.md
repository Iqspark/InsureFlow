# General FAQ — InsureFlow Cyber Liability Insurance

## What is cyber liability insurance?
Cyber liability insurance covers a business against the financial costs of a data breach or cyber attack. Typical protection includes incident response and forensics, data restoration, business interruption following an outage, ransomware and extortion costs, regulatory defence and penalties, and third-party liability arising from a breach of customer or partner data. In short, it helps a business respond to and recover from a cyber event without absorbing the full cost itself.

## Who needs cyber liability insurance?
Any business that stores sensitive data, relies on connected systems, or processes electronic payments has cyber exposure. This policy is especially relevant for businesses in professional services, retail and e-commerce, healthcare, financial services, manufacturing, and technology / SaaS. The more sensitive records a business holds and the larger its revenue, the greater its exposure to a costly incident.

## How is the premium calculated?
The base annual premium is 1.2% of the chosen aggregate limit (for example, $12,000 CAD on a $1,000,000 limit). That base is then adjusted by a series of factors:
- Industry threat profile (Professional Services is the baseline; Retail, Healthcare, Financial, Manufacturing, Technology, and Other each carry their own factor)
- Annual revenue band (micro through enterprise)
- Volume of sensitive records held
- Whether MFA is enforced
- Data backup strategy
- Endpoint security in place
- Prior cyber incidents in the last 5 years
- Deductible selected

Two flat dollar loadings may also apply after the multipliers: $1,000 CAD if MFA is not enforced, and $750 CAD if no endpoint protection is deployed. Strong security controls (enforced MFA, tested offline/immutable backups, EDR) reduce the premium, while weaker controls and a heavier loss history increase it.

## What aggregate limits are available?
The aggregate limit is the most we will pay across all claims in the policy year. Available limits are:
- $250,000 CAD
- $500,000 CAD
- $1,000,000 CAD (most popular)
- $3,000,000 CAD
- $5,000,000 CAD

## What deductible options are available?
Deductibles are offered at $5,000, $10,000, $25,000, and $50,000 CAD. A higher deductible lowers the premium but increases the out-of-pocket cost per claim. $10,000 CAD is the most popular, balanced option.

## What security controls do you ask about and why?
We ask about three core controls because they are the strongest predictors of whether a business can prevent or recover from an attack:
- **Multi-factor authentication (MFA)** — enforced across email, remote access, and admin accounts. MFA is the single most effective control against account takeover and ransomware, and it directly lowers the premium when enforced.
- **Data backups** — tested offline or immutable backups are the key to recovering from ransomware without paying, and earn the best rate; regular backups are standard.
- **Endpoint security** — EDR / managed detection earns the best rate, standard antivirus is treated as standard cover.

Weaker controls raise the premium and, in some cases, trigger underwriter review or a decline (see below).

## What can cause a quote to be declined or referred?
A quote is **declined** when:
- The business has no data backups — without backups it cannot recover from ransomware, which falls outside our underwriting appetite.

A quote is **referred** to an underwriter for individual review when any of the following apply:
- Industry is Healthcare (regulated health information / PHI exposure)
- Industry is Financial (heightened regulatory and threat-actor exposure)
- Annual revenue exceeds $250,000,000 CAD (requires senior approval and a detailed risk survey)
- The business holds more than 1,000,000 sensitive records (large aggregation exposure)
- MFA is not enforced (a baseline control)
- No endpoint protection is deployed
- The business has had 3 or more cyber incidents in the last 5 years

A referral is not a decline — it means an underwriter reviews the risk before a final decision.
