# Cancellations & Refunds

## How do I cancel a policy?
Contact your assigned broker to request cancellation. The broker submits a cancellation request to the insurer with the effective date.

## Will I get a refund if I cancel mid-term?
Possibly. A mid-term cancellation is usually calculated on a **short-rate** basis, meaning the refund is the unused portion of the premium minus a cancellation penalty — so it's a bit less than a simple pro-rata refund. The exact amount depends on how much of the 12-month term has elapsed.

## What's the difference between short-rate and pro-rata?
- **Pro-rata** — refund is exactly proportional to the unused time.
- **Short-rate** — refund is the unused time minus a penalty (common when the policyholder cancels). The short-rate table sets the percentage retained.

## Can the insurer cancel my policy?
Yes, under the policy's statutory conditions (for example for non-payment or material misrepresentation), with the required notice. Your broker can explain the specifics for your province.

## What happens to a policy that's bound but not yet paid?
If the customer hasn't completed payment, the policy isn't active. The broker can resend the payment link or let it lapse. There's nothing to refund because no payment was taken.

## Does cancelling delete my record?
No. A bound policy's record is retained for history and compliance — cancellation changes its status, it isn't erased.

## How do renewals relate to cancellation?
Each policy runs a 12-month term. If you don't want to renew, simply let your broker know before the expiry date shown in Upcoming Renewals; cancelling mid-term (before expiry) is the short-rate process above.

## Who do I contact?
Always start with your assigned broker — they handle cancellation requests, refund calculations, and any provincial statutory requirements.
