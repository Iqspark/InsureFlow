# General FAQ — InsureFlow Architects & Engineers Insurance

## What is architects & engineers insurance?
Architects & Engineers insurance is a Professional Indemnity (PI) policy that protects a design practice against claims of professional negligence arising from its design services and advice. If a client alleges that an error, omission, or breach of professional duty in the firm's drawings, calculations, specifications, or recommendations caused them financial loss, the policy responds to the cost of defending and settling that claim.

Professional Indemnity differs from general liability: it covers the economic consequences of mistakes in professional work (errors and omissions), not bodily injury or property damage from ordinary operations. Many public-sector and large private clients contractually require design firms to carry a minimum PI limit before awarding work.

## Who needs it?
This policy is intended for architecture and engineering practices, including:
- Architecture firms
- Structural engineering firms
- Civil engineering firms
- Mechanical / electrical engineering firms
- Multi-discipline design practices

## How is the premium calculated?
The annual premium is driven by the firm's **annual fee income** at a base rate of **5.0%** of fee income for a baseline, well-run, low-hazard practice. That base premium is then adjusted by a series of risk multipliers:
- **Fee income band** — larger firms carry larger and more complex projects
- **Discipline** — weighted for professional-negligence hazard by area of practice
- **Province** — weighted for local construction-litigation climate
- **Practice experience** — years the firm has been practising
- **Work mix** — share of structural / forensic work
- **Quality assurance** — whether a documented QA / peer-review process is in place
- **Claims history** — prior PI claims in the last 5 years
- **PI limit** — the coverage limit selected
- **Deductible** — the per-claim deductible chosen

Firms with a documented QA process, a long track record, and a clean claims history receive the most favourable rates.

## What coverage limits are available?
The Professional Indemnity limit is the most we would pay on a single claim. Available limits (CAD) are:
- $250,000 — lowest premium
- $500,000
- $1,000,000 — most popular
- $2,000,000
- $5,000,000 — highest premium

## What deductible options are available?
The deductible is the amount the firm pays out-of-pocket per claim. A higher deductible lowers the premium. Options (CAD) are:
- $5,000 — higher premium
- $10,000 — balanced, most popular
- $25,000 — lower premium
- $50,000 — lowest premium

## What can cause a quote to be declined or referred?
The quoting engine refers a quote for individual underwriter review (rather than declining outright) in the following cases:
- The firm is based in the **Northwest Territories, Nunavut, or Yukon** (remote project conditions and limited local infrastructure)
- The primary discipline is **structural** or **civil engineering** (higher-hazard load-bearing and infrastructure design)
- **Annual fee income exceeds $25,000,000 CAD** (requires senior underwriter approval and a project-profile review)
- The firm has been practising **under 2 years** (review of principals' track record and supervision)
- More than **50% of work** is structural or forensic / expert-witness engagements (high-hazard work mix)
- The firm has **no documented QA / peer-review process**
- The firm has had **3 or more** professional-liability claims or circumstances in the last 5 years

A referred quote is reviewed by an underwriter rather than bound automatically.
