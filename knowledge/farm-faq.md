# General FAQ — InsureFlow Farm Insurance

## What is farm insurance?
Farm insurance is a multi-section package that protects a farm operation's dwellings, outbuildings (barns, machine sheds, grain storage), machinery and equipment, livestock, and farm liability. It mirrors a full farm application — covering the home, the buildings, the equipment, the animals, the earnings, and the liability exposure — in one policy.

## Who needs farm insurance?
Any owner or operator of a working or hobby farm — crop/grain, livestock, dairy, mixed, or lifestyle/hobby farms. It suits operations that have a farm dwelling, outbuildings, machinery, and/or livestock to insure, plus the liability that comes with running a farm.

## What information do I need to get a quote?
The application is organized into modules, so you'll be asked about:
- **General Information** — named insured, mailing address, province, current insurer, years insured, operation type, years operating, any prior refusal/cancellation
- **Locations** — number of locations, acres, owned/rented, additional locations
- **Habitational (main dwelling)** — occupancy, year built, square footage, construction, roof type & age, electrical wiring & amperage, plumbing, primary heating (and oil-tank detail), wood/solid-fuel heat & bush cords, smoke detectors, monitored security, swimming pool, coverage form, guaranteed replacement cost
- **Farm Buildings** — number of buildings, total value, primary use
- **Machinery & Equipment** — total value, valuation basis (RC/ACV), high-value units, custom farming for others
- **Livestock** — type, count, value, whether you board others' animals, loss-prevention measures
- **Earnings & Profits** — gross annual receipts, US/international sales, agritourism/public access, unlicensed cannabis use
- **Tank Data** — storage tanks over 500 gallons (product, capacity, containment)
- **Liability** — liability limit, clandestine lab, chemical handling for others, environmental compliance, bodies of water
- **Loss History** — losses in the last 5 years (cause and largest amount)
- **Property & Coverage** — total sum insured, deductible
- **Broker Information** — contact, broker code, narrative notes

## How is the premium calculated?
The base annual premium is driven by the **total sum insured** (dwellings + outbuildings + equipment + livestock) at a base rate of about 0.6%. That base is then adjusted by risk factors including operation type, province, operating experience, gross revenue, dwelling age/construction/roof/wiring/plumbing/heating, fire protection and security, swimming pool, building schedule, liability limit, and loss history, plus the chosen deductible. Flat loadings may apply for certified wood heat, agritourism, and boarding others' livestock.

## What liability limits are available?
$1 Million, $2 Million (most popular), or $5 Million.

## What deductible options are available?
$1,000, $2,500 (balanced — most popular), $5,000, or $10,000 CAD. A higher deductible lowers the premium.

## What can cause a quote to be declined or referred?
A quote is **declined** when:
- The premises has been used to grow cannabis beyond the legal household limit without a licence
- There is a clandestine (synthetic drug) lab on the premises

A quote is **referred** to an underwriter when, for example: the farm is in a remote territory (NT/NU/YT); it's a new operation (under 1 year); there's a prior refusal/cancellation; the dwelling is vacant or built before 1920; the roof is over 25 years; wiring is knob-and-tube; plumbing is Poly-B; the oil tank is single-walled or 15+ years; wood heat is uncertified; there are no smoke detectors; the pool is unfenced; there's custom farming for others, livestock boarding, US/international sales, agritourism, chemical handling for others, or known environmental non-compliance; storage tanks exceed 500 gallons; the sum insured exceeds $10,000,000; or there are 3+ losses (or a single loss over $500,000) in 5 years.

A referral is not a decline — an underwriter reviews the risk before deciding.

## What is the policy term?
All policies are issued for a 12-month term, with the renewal date tracked in the portal.

## Can I cancel mid-term?
Yes. Contact your assigned broker to request cancellation. A short-rate penalty may apply.
