# Coverage & Common Exclusions

## What does "exclusion" mean?
An exclusion is something the policy does not cover. Every policy lists exclusions and endorsements that modify the standard wording. The detailed clauses are available in the knowledge base as policy-wording documents.

## What exclusions are commonly applied?
Across our products, common exclusions and endorsements include:
- **War and civil war** — losses caused by war, invasion, or insurrection.
- **Terrorism** — loss arising from acts of terrorism (per the terrorism exclusion endorsement).
- **Nuclear / radioactive contamination** — nuclear incidents and radioactive contamination.
- **Cyber & data** — cyber and data losses are excluded from property/liability wordings (cyber risk is covered by the dedicated Cyber Liability product).
- **Pollution, seepage & contamination** — gradual pollution, seepage, land/air/water contamination.
- **Communicable disease** — loss or liability arising from communicable disease (endorsement/exclusion).
- **Asbestos, silica, biological or chemical materials** — health-hazard materials.
- **Sexual harassment / abuse** — excluded from general liability.
- **Electromagnetic fields (EMF)** — for public/products liability.
- **Vandalism / unoccupancy conditions** — vacant/unoccupied properties carry specific conditions and exclusions (see the Vacant Home product).
- **Sanctions** — cover does not apply where it would breach sanctions law.
- **North American / USA operations** — certain wordings exclude or condition US exposure.

The exact clauses, definitions, and any buy-back endorsements are in the corresponding policy-wording PDFs in the knowledge base.

## Are floods and sewer backup covered?
These are typically handled by endorsement — a **Flood Endorsement** and a **Sewer Backup Endorsement** can extend cover where eligible. Ask your broker whether they apply to a given risk.

## What about water damage on a vacant home?
Vacant and unoccupied properties have specific **unoccupancy conditions** and a supervisory warranty — for example requirements around inspections and winterizing utilities. Not meeting them can affect a water-damage claim.

## How do I know what a specific policy covers?
Open the quote or policy in the portal and download the branded PDF for the full details, then refer to the relevant wording documents. For anything specific, your broker can confirm exactly what applies.

## Does the quote tell me why something was declined?
Yes. Decline and refer reasons are shown on the quote result screen and stored on the submission, so the broker (and underwriter) can see exactly which rule applied.
