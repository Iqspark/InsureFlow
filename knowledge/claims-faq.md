# Claims — Frequently Asked Questions

## How do I file a claim?
Contact your assigned broker as soon as possible after a loss. The broker reports the claim to the insurer and guides you through the next steps. For urgent situations (fire, major water damage, theft), take steps to prevent further damage and keep receipts for any emergency expenses.

## What information should I have ready?
- Your policy / application ID
- The date and a description of what happened
- Photos or video of the damage if it's safe to take them
- A list of damaged or stolen items with approximate values
- Any police or fire report number (for theft, vandalism, or fire)
- Receipts or proof of ownership for higher-value items

## How quickly should I report a loss?
Report it promptly — policies require timely notice. Delaying a report can affect the claim.

## What happens after I report a claim?
The insurer assigns the claim, may appoint an adjuster to assess the damage, and works with you on the settlement based on your coverage, limits, and deductible. Your deductible is applied to the settlement.

## Will a claim affect my premium?
Loss history is a rating factor on renewal and on future quotes — a heavier claims record can increase the premium or trigger an underwriter review.

## Does the portal track my claims?
The InsureFlow portal handles quoting, binding, and payment. Claims are handled by your broker and the insurer directly — start with your broker.

## What's not covered?
Every policy has exclusions (for example war, nuclear, certain pollution, and others depending on the product). See "Coverage & Common Exclusions" and your policy wording for specifics.

## I'm a broker — where do I see the client's policy details for a claim?
Open the policy from your dashboard, Policies, or Search. You can download a branded PDF of the full policy/quote details to support the claim.
