# Paying for Your Policy — Customer FAQ

## I received a payment link from my broker. Is it legitimate?
If your broker told you to expect it, yes — after your quote is approved and the broker binds the policy, InsureFlow emails you a secure link to pay on our checkout page. If you're unsure, confirm with your broker before paying.

## How do I pay?
Open the link in the email — it takes you to a **secure Stripe checkout** page where you enter your card details and pay. You don't need to log in; the link is unique to your policy.

## Do I need an account to pay?
No. The payment page is public and tied to your specific policy via a secure token in the link.

## What happens after I pay?
Once your payment is confirmed, your policy shows **Paid** and activates, and you'll receive a confirmation and a **receipt by email with your policy document (PDF) attached**. The broker also sees the policy marked as paid.

## I didn't get the payment email — what now?
Check your spam/junk folder first. If it's not there, ask your broker to resend the link — they can do this from the policy page or their dashboard.

## The link doesn't work, is invalid, or says "This link has expired."
Payment links **expire 30 days after they're issued** for security. If your link has expired or won't open, ask your broker to **resend** it — they issue a fresh link with a new 30-day window. Links are tied to your policy and shouldn't be shared.

## Is my card actually charged?
When card processing is enabled, yes — payment is taken securely through **Stripe** and your policy is marked Paid only after Stripe confirms it. For demo setups, the checkout may be a simulated page that validates your card format without charging; your broker can confirm the live arrangement.

## Can I get a copy of my policy document?
Yes. The receipt email includes your branded policy PDF, and you can also download it any time from your **customer portal** link (see "Customer Self-Service Portal").

## When does my coverage start?
Coverage activates once payment is confirmed. Your policy runs for a 12-month term from its effective date.

## Who do I contact with payment questions?
Your assigned broker is your point of contact for anything about the quote, the policy, or the payment link.
