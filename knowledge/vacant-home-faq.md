# General FAQ — InsureFlow Vacant Home Insurance

## What is vacant home insurance?
Vacant home insurance is a specialist policy that covers a residential property that is unoccupied for 30 consecutive days or more. Standard home insurance policies typically exclude or limit coverage once a property is vacant, so a dedicated vacant home policy fills that gap.

## Who needs vacant home insurance?
You may need this policy if:
- You are selling a property that has already been vacated
- You have inherited a property and are managing the estate
- You are renovating a home before moving in or renting it out
- You are temporarily away (travelling, hospitalization, extended work assignment)
- You own a seasonal property that is empty for part of the year

## What provinces do you operate in?
InsureFlow writes vacant home policies in all Canadian provinces and territories, including Ontario, British Columbia, Alberta, Quebec, Manitoba, Saskatchewan, Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, Prince Edward Island, Newfoundland and Labrador, Northwest Territories, Nunavut, and Yukon.

## How long does a quote take?
Our online quoting tool produces an instant quote in under five minutes. A broker confirms the policy within one business day.

## What information do I need to get a quote?
- Applicant name and contact email
- Property province
- Property type (detached, semi-detached, townhouse, condo)
- Year built and square footage
- Estimated replacement value
- How long the property has been vacant and why
- Whether utilities are winterized
- Security features (alarm, deadbolts, etc.)
- Any prior damage or claims in the last five years

## How is the premium calculated?
The base annual premium starts at $800 CAD and is adjusted by factors including:
- Province of risk
- Property age and construction type
- Length and reason for vacancy
- Coverage amount selected
- Deductible chosen
- Security and maintenance measures
- Prior claims history

## What are the available coverage amounts?
Coverage is offered as a percentage of the property's replacement value: 80%, 90%, or 100%.

## What deductible options are available?
Standard deductibles are $1,000, $2,500, and $5,000 CAD.

## What is the policy term?
All policies are issued for a 12-month term.

## Can I cancel mid-term?
Yes. Contact your assigned broker to request cancellation. A short-rate penalty may apply.
