# General FAQ — InsureFlow Vacant Home Insurance

## What is vacant home insurance?
Vacant home insurance is a specialist policy that covers a residential property that is unoccupied for 30 consecutive days or more. Standard home insurance policies typically exclude or limit coverage once a property is vacant, so a dedicated vacant home policy fills that gap.

## Who needs vacant home insurance?
You may need this policy if:
- You are selling a property that has already been vacated
- You have inherited a property and are managing the estate
- You are renovating a home before moving in or renting it out
- You are temporarily away (travelling, hospitalization, extended work assignment)
- You own a seasonal property that is empty for part of the year

## What provinces do you operate in?
InsureFlow writes vacant home policies in all Canadian provinces and territories, including Ontario, British Columbia, Alberta, Quebec, Manitoba, Saskatchewan, Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, Prince Edward Island, Newfoundland and Labrador, Northwest Territories, Nunavut, and Yukon.

## How long does a quote take?
Our online quoting tool produces an instant quote in under five minutes. A broker confirms the policy within one business day.

## What information do I need to get a quote?
- Applicant name, phone, and email
- Property address (the province is derived automatically from the address)
- Property type (single family, townhouse, condo/apartment, multi-family 2–4 units, or mobile/manufactured)
- Year built and approximate square footage
- Estimated replacement (rebuild) cost
- Dwelling coverage level (80%, 90%, or 100% of replacement cost)
- Deductible
- How long the property has been vacant and the reason for the vacancy
- How frequently someone inspects the property
- Whether the utilities have been shut off / winterized
- Security measures in place (alarm, deadbolts, etc.)
- Whether there is a swimming pool (and if so, whether it is fenced)
- Any known existing damage (and its type)
- Number of insurance claims in the last 5 years (with cause, repair status, and size for each)
- Whether the property is currently or recently insured
- Roof age
- Whether electrical and plumbing have been updated within the last 25 years
- Primary heating source
- Fire protection (distance to hydrant and fire hall)
- Whether any renovation or construction is in progress
- Whether the site is fenced or otherwise secured

## How is the premium calculated?
The base annual premium starts at $500 CAD. It is then multiplied by a series of rating factors and, finally, a few flat dollar loadings are added. The multipliers applied are:
- Province / territory of risk
- Length of vacancy
- Property type
- Replacement cost (scales upward above a $350,000 baseline)
- Property age (based on year built)
- Inspection frequency
- Security measures
- Prior claims history
- Deductible
- Coverage level (% of replacement cost)
- Roof age
- Electrical / plumbing updates
- Heating type
- Fire protection
- Renovation in progress
- Whether the site is fenced / secured

The following flat loadings (CAD) are then added where they apply: $200 for an unfenced pool, $150 for known existing damage, $100 for a lapse in prior insurance, and $75 for active (un-winterized) utilities. The premium is also shown monthly (annual divided by 12).

## What can cause a quote to be declined or referred?
A quote is **declined** (outside underwriting appetite) when:
- The property is a mobile / manufactured home
- The property has been vacant for more than 5 years

A quote is **referred** to an underwriter for individual review when:
- The property is in the Northwest Territories, Nunavut, or Yukon
- The property was built before 1900
- The replacement cost exceeds $3,000,000 CAD
- The property has been vacant 3–5 years
- No one regularly inspects the property
- The utilities are still active (not winterized)
- No security measures are in place
- There is an unfenced / accessible swimming pool
- There is major structural, fire, or water damage
- There have been 3 or more claims in the last 5 years, a prior fire/smoke loss, unrepaired claim damage, or a single claim over $50,000 CAD
- The property has had a lapse in coverage
- The roof is more than 30 years old
- The primary heating is wood / solid fuel
- There is no nearby fire hydrant or fire hall
- A major structural renovation is in progress

## What are the available coverage amounts?
Coverage is offered as a percentage of the property's replacement value: 80%, 90%, or 100%.

## What deductible options are available?
Standard deductibles are $1,000, $2,500, and $5,000 CAD.

## What is the policy term?
All policies are issued for a 12-month term.

## Can I cancel mid-term?
Yes. Contact your assigned broker to request cancellation. A short-rate penalty may apply.
