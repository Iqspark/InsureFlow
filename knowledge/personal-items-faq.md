# General FAQ — InsureFlow Personal Items Insurance

## What is personal items insurance?
Personal items insurance — also called scheduled valuable articles cover — insures individually listed high-value personal possessions such as jewellery, watches, fine art, collectibles, cameras, musical instruments, and sports equipment against loss, theft, and damage. Each article is named and valued on a schedule, so cover is tailored to the specific item rather than capped by the sub-limits found in a standard home policy.

In the broader market this type of cover (often grouped under "fine art and specie") is typically written on an all-risk, worldwide basis for named items, meaning the listed articles are protected against most causes of loss wherever they are — at home or while being carried. InsureFlow's product schedules each item to its appraised or replacement value and prices the policy from the total sum insured.

## Who needs it?
You may want a scheduled personal items policy if you:
- Own fine jewellery or watches whose value exceeds standard home-policy sub-limits
- Collect fine art, antiques, or other collectibles
- Carry valuable cameras, electronics, or musical instruments away from home
- Use high-value sports equipment
- Want full-value, agreed cover on specific articles rather than a single contents limit

## How is the premium calculated?
The base annual premium is **1.5% of the total insured value** (your sum insured). That base is then adjusted by a series of multipliers and flat dollar loadings:
- **Item category** — sports equipment 0.95, cameras/electronics and "other" 1.00, collectibles and musical instruments 1.05, jewellery/watches 1.10, fine art 1.20
- **Province / location** — ranging from 0.95 (PE) up to 1.18 (BC), with remote territories highest (NT 1.30, NU 1.35, YT 1.25)
- **Storage security** — bank vault/safe deposit box 0.80, home safe + monitored alarm 0.95, monitored alarm only 1.20, no special security 1.50
- **Professional appraisal** — appraised within 3 years 0.95, otherwise 1.20
- **Carried outside the home** — a 1.25 multiplier applied only when items are regularly carried or worn off-premises
- **Loss history (5 yrs)** — none 0.95, one loss 1.15, two losses 1.35, three or more 1.60
- **Deductible** — $0 1.15, $500 1.00, $1,000 0.92, $2,500 0.82
- **Number of scheduled items** — 1–3 0.95, 4–10 1.00, 11–25 1.10, 26 or more 1.20
- **Worn / used frequency** — kept in a vault 0.85, special occasions only 1.00, weekly 1.15, every day 1.30
- **Monitored alarm** — central station monitored 0.90, self/app monitored 1.05, no alarm 1.25
- **International travel** — never 1.00, occasionally 1.15, frequently 1.35
- **Prior theft of valuables** — a 1.30 multiplier applied only when a prior theft is reported
- **Documentation on file** — photos/serials/engravings on file 0.92, otherwise 1.10

Two flat dollar loadings are added after the multipliers: **$150 CAD** when any single item is valued over $50,000, and **$100 CAD** when items are regularly carried off-premises. The annual premium is also shown as a monthly figure (one twelfth of the annual amount).

## What coverage limits are available?
There is no fixed menu of coverage tiers — you set your own sum insured equal to the total value of the items you schedule. The total insured value must be between **$5,000 and $2,000,000 CAD**, and the value of the single most expensive item can be entered up to **$2,000,000 CAD**. High totals and high single-item values trigger underwriter referral (see below).

## What deductible options are available?
Four deductible options are offered, all in CAD:
- **$0** — higher premium
- **$500** — balanced and most popular
- **$1,000** — lower premium
- **$2,500** — lowest premium

A higher deductible lowers your premium but increases your out-of-pocket cost per claim.

## What can cause a quote to be declined or referred?
The following all trigger an underwriter referral (refer) rather than an instant decision:
- Home located in the **Northwest Territories, Nunavut, or Yukon** (remoteness and limited security/response infrastructure)
- **Fine art** schedules — these require individual review and a specialist appraisal before terms can be offered
- A **total scheduled value over $500,000 CAD** — requires senior underwriter approval
- A **single item valued over $100,000 CAD** — requires review and a current specialist appraisal
- **No professional appraisal within the last 3 years** — values must be confirmed
- Items kept with **no special security** (no safe or monitored alarm)
- **Three or more** loss, theft, or damage claims in the last 5 years
- A prior loss **over $100,000 CAD**
- Items taken on international trips **frequently** (worldwide territory coverage reviewed)
- Any **prior theft of valuables** (whether insured or not)

A referral is not a decline — it means a broker or underwriter reviews the submission before offering terms.
