# General FAQ — InsureFlow Lithium Battery Product Liability Insurance

## What is lithium battery product liability insurance?
Lithium battery product liability insurance protects businesses in the lithium-battery supply chain against third-party claims arising from a defective battery product. Product liability covers the costs of legal defence and settlements when a product causes bodily injury to a person or damage to someone else's property — for lithium batteries this most commonly means thermal-runaway events, fires, and recall-driven losses. It is a specialist line because lithium cells carry a fire and explosion hazard that general liability cover often limits or excludes.

## Who needs it?
This policy is designed for businesses that make, brand, move, or sell lithium-battery products, including:
- Manufacturers of cells or battery packs
- Importers and brand owners
- Distributors and wholesalers
- Resellers and retailers

Businesses based in the Northwest Territories, Nunavut, or Yukon require individual underwriter review.

## What information do I need to get a quote?
- Contact name and the legal name of the business
- Province or territory the business is based in
- Role in the supply chain (manufacturer, importer/brand owner, distributor/wholesaler, reseller/retailer)
- Battery chemistry (Li-ion, Li-polymer, LiFePO4, lithium-metal, or mixed)
- Primary end-use application (consumer electronics, power tools, e-mobility, EV/automotive, energy storage, or other)
- Annual turnover (in CAD) attributable to the lithium-battery products
- Safety certification held by the cells and packs
- Whether any product has been recalled in the last 5 years
- Number of product liability claims in the last 5 years
- Desired aggregate limit and deductible
- Business phone and email

## How is the premium calculated?
The premium is turnover-rated. The base annual premium is 0.8% of the annual turnover attributable to the lithium-battery products, then adjusted by risk multipliers and a flat loading:
- Supply-chain role
- Province of the business
- Battery chemistry
- End-use application
- Turnover band
- Safety certification
- Recall history (last 5 years)
- Prior product liability claims (last 5 years)
- Deductible chosen

A flat loading of $1,500 CAD is added if there has been a product recall in the last 5 years.

## What coverage limits are available?
The product liability aggregate limit — the most we would pay in total for product liability claims in a policy period — is offered at:
- $1,000,000 CAD (entry limit)
- $2,000,000 CAD (most popular)
- $5,000,000 CAD
- $10,000,000 CAD

## What deductible options are available?
Deductibles are $5,000, $10,000, $25,000, and $50,000 CAD. A higher deductible lowers the premium but increases the out-of-pocket cost per claim.

## What can cause a quote to be declined or referred?
A quote is **declined** when:
- The product holds no safety certification (uncertified products are outside our underwriting appetite)

A quote is **referred** to an underwriter for individual review when:
- The business is based in the Northwest Territories, Nunavut, or Yukon (remoteness and limited recall infrastructure)
- The business is a manufacturer or importer/brand owner (carries primary product liability)
- The battery chemistry is lithium-metal (primary) — a higher-hazard chemistry
- The end-use application is e-mobility (e-bikes/scooters) or EV/automotive (high recall and bodily-injury exposure)
- Annual turnover exceeds $50,000,000 CAD (senior underwriter approval and portfolio review)
- Products are self-certified or CE-only without independent UL/IEC certification
- There has been a product recall in the last 5 years (review of root cause and corrective actions)
- The business has had 3 or more product liability claims in the last 5 years
