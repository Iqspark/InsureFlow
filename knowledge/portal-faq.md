# Portal FAQ — Common Questions

## How long does a quote take?
The chat-style questionnaire produces an instant decision and premium in under five minutes.

## Which products can I quote?
Ten: Vacant Home, Rental Home, Farm, Jeweller's Block, Cyber Liability, Contractor, Architects & Engineers, Retailers, Personal Items, and Lithium Battery Product Liability.

## What's the difference between a quote and a policy?
A **quote** is a saved, calculated application. It becomes a **policy** when the broker binds it (presses Buy This Policy). A policy is **paid** once the customer completes payment.

## Why was my quote referred instead of accepted?
A referral means the risk needs an underwriter's judgement before a decision — for example a high-value risk, a heavier loss history, or a risk factor outside the automatic-accept range. The specific reasons are shown on the result screen. A referral is not a decline.

## Can I change a policy after it's bound?
Yes. A broker or admin can make a **mid-term adjustment** to revise the coverage amount (the premium changes pro-rata for the remaining term) or **cancel** the policy — both from the policy page, and both email the customer a confirmation. See "Mid-Term Adjustments" and "Cancellations & Refunds."

## Can I change an answer after I've moved on?
Yes. Go back to any earlier question and change it; the questionnaire re-walks the path and keeps your still-relevant answers, so you don't lose later ones like phone and email.

## How do I find a specific policy or client?
- **Policies** — search by customer name or policy number with type-ahead suggestions.
- **Customers** — search by name or email to open a client's full history.
- **Search** — filter all submissions by name, application ID, date, policy type, or stage.

## Can I export my data?
Yes — the dashboard, Policies, and Search pages have an **Export CSV** button (scoped to what your role can see).

## How is my premium worked out?
Each product has a base premium (driven by the sum insured, limit, or revenue) adjusted by risk factors from your answers, plus the chosen deductible. The result screen shows the full factor breakdown.

## What deductible and limit options are there?
They vary by product and are presented as choices during the quote. Higher deductibles lower the premium; higher limits raise it.

## Is my data secure and role-scoped?
Yes. Brokers see only their own quotes, policies, and customers. Underwriters and admins see across all brokers per their role. Every list, search, and export goes through role-based access control.

## What if the Help Navigator can't answer?
If a question is outside the knowledge base, the assistant says it's out of scope rather than guessing. Contact your administrator for anything portal- or account-specific.
